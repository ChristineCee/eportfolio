Goal 2

Understand the essential nature of information management and its relevance to society

Outcome A

Describe various definitions and concepts of information

During our very first IM 701 all-day session, our MSIM cohort discussed the following topics:

  • What is the Difference Between Data, Information, and Knowledge? (And, Why Should We Care?)
  • Perspectives on the Social and Cultural Impact of Technology
  • Theories as Tools to Explain Information Creation and Use

In-class exercises were completed, which not only served to introduce us to the various definitions and concepts of information, but also to each other as classmates. This was invaluable, as we went through the two-year sequence of MSIM courses together and frequently worked in pairs, small groups, and as a cohort team.

Our day-long introduction to information definitions, information creation and the impact of technology informed my choice of topic for my Issues/Trends Presentation, as I learned about digital assets, researched and wrote about digital asset management systems.

Artifact
IM 701, Issue/Trends Presentation

Outcome B

Articulate the various lifecycle stages for data, metadata and information

Throughout the courses for the MSIM program, we discussed various aspects of the information lifecycle (creation, publication and retention/removal), as related to the particular course topic. I found this particularly helpful during IM 703, Information Architecture, while working on my information strategic plan. The information strategic plan outlined the goals of my fictional organization, Sam’s Fresh Market, new projects and initiatives of the organization, the nature of the information required for the operational needs and any new projects.  This document served as the basis for several later assignments and projects, such as my interface design for IM 760, Human-Computer Interaction.

Artifact
IM 703 Information Strategic Plan

Outcome C

Articulate the context of information management and the social, ethical, cultural, legal and economic factors that impact information systems

Assignment: IM 703 Online Governance Discussion

The assignment: Choose one or more of the readings for this week. Write a posting reflecting on the following questions:

  • Identify one important concept that you learned  
  • Why do you believe that this concept is important?
  • How does this reading increase your understanding of systems?
  • What question(s) has the reading raised for you?

For this assignment, I chose Jonathan Kahn's article, "Web Governance: Becoming an Agent of Change,"from the assigned readings. My online discussion focused on the challenge of web governance in organizations where there are many stakeholders in a company’s website (for example, marketing and IT), how these departments can become “siloed” and how siloed departments can share information and institutional knowledge to better collaborate on web governance.
